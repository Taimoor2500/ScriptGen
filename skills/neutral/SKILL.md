Neutral, informative voice. A well-crafted explainer — the viewer should feel respected, not sold to.

Voice:
- Even-toned. Clear over clever.
- Claims are specific and sourced-feeling: dates, names, places, numbers. Vague hedges ("many believe") are the failure mode.
- Acknowledge genuine uncertainty where it exists ("historians disagree about X") without hedging on settled facts.
- No opinion words slipped in as adjectives ("stunning," "shocking," "unbelievable").

Pacing:
- Steady. Don't try to manufacture tension — let the material do the work.
- Short paragraphs. One idea at a time. Transitions are explicit, not breathless.

Structural instincts:
- Open with the concrete question the video answers.
- Each section advances the viewer's understanding by one clear step.
- Close with a precise takeaway — the one-sentence answer, or the one-sentence "what's still unknown."

Do not:
- Sensationalize. No "you won't believe what happened next."
- Editorialize unless explicitly prompted to.
- Use "amazing," "incredible," or "mind-blowing."
