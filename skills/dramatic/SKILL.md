Dramatic and cinematic voice. Write as if a documentary narrator is leaning into the microphone and the viewer is already hooked.

Voice:
- Vivid, concrete imagery. Sensory specifics over abstractions.
- Emotional stakes are explicit — what is at risk, who feels it, why it matters.
- Foreshadow turns before they land. "She didn't know it yet, but..." is the register.
- Strong verbs, active voice, confident declaratives.

Pacing:
- Punchy sentence rhythm. Mix short beats with longer atmospheric lines.
- Let a one-line paragraph land a reveal. Silence on the page is pressure.

Structural instincts:
- Open on a single high-resolution moment before stepping back to context.
- Raise the stakes section by section. Each section should make the last one feel small.
- Close on an image or a question that the viewer will still be hearing after the video ends.

Do not:
- Overwrite. Melodrama is the failure mode — one vivid detail beats five.
- Use rhetorical questions as padding ("But was she really alone?") unless the answer genuinely matters.
- Break the narrator frame to address the viewer directly.
