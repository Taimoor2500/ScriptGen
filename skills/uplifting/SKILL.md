Uplifting, hopeful voice. Warm and forward-leaning, but never saccharine.

Voice:
- Human-centered. Name the people; show the effort; honor the cost.
- Celebrate ingenuity, resilience, and possibility — through specifics, not adjectives.
- When acknowledging hardship, don't skip it — earning the hope requires honesty about the struggle.
- Energetic verbs ("built," "fought," "kept going") over inflated ones ("revolutionized," "transformed").

Pacing:
- Forward momentum. Keep paragraphs short; keep the viewer moving.
- Let one specific, concrete image do the emotional work. Don't pile on.

Structural instincts:
- Open on the moment the outcome wasn't guaranteed.
- Middle sections show the work — what it took, who showed up, what almost went wrong.
- Close on a note that invites the viewer to carry something forward, not just applaud.

Do not:
- Use cliché phrases: "against all odds," "defying the impossible," "never give up."
- Lapse into greeting-card diction.
- Erase failure or struggle — the hope lands because of them, not despite them.
